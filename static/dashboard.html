⚠ SQC DETERMINATION: Extended survey triggered. Hot water temps corrected during survey (2/17/2026). Readings up to 137°F in Room 142.

1. Corrective Action for Identified Resident(s)

Hot Water Temperatures (IMMEDIATE — Corrected During Survey):
On 2/11/2026, upon identification of water temperatures exceeding safe limits (readings up to 137°F in Room 142), the Administrator immediately ordered the boiler temperature lowered and hot water shut off temporarily to affected units.
A licensed plumbing company was contracted and completed repairs, including installation/calibration of thermostatic mixing valves, by 2/17/2026.
Post-repair temperature readings were taken in all resident bathrooms and confirmed within the safe range (100–110°F) before hot water service was restored.
All residents on affected units were assessed for burns or skin injury. No adverse outcomes were identified.

Physical Environment Repairs:
Resident #127: Loose shower threshold was repaired; shower chair with jagged protrusion was immediately removed and replaced; cracked/discolored sink scheduled for replacement.
Residents #66 and #90: Unsanded spackle patches sanded, primed, and painted; wall gouges repaired; cracked/stained sink and toilet scheduled for replacement; gap under sink pipes sealed.
Resident #1: Extensive drywall damage and holes around sink piping repaired, sealed, and painted.
Resident #8: Paint/drywall repaired; dresser handle replaced; cracked/discolored sink scheduled for replacement.
Resident #40: Cracked, stained, slow-draining sink cleared and scheduled for replacement.

Linen Shortages:
Resident #140 and all affected residents received immediate supply of towels, washcloths, and soap.
The laundry service was assessed for capacity, turnaround time, and par levels. An emergency linen order was placed to supplement existing inventory.

2. Identification of Other Residents Potentially Affected

Hot Water: Hot water temperatures were measured in every resident room and common area bathroom facility-wide. All rooms with readings above 110°F were identified and corrected prior to restoring service. All residents were assessed for burn injuries — none identified.
Physical Environment: The Maintenance Director and Administrator conducted a comprehensive room-by-room environmental assessment of all 182 licensed beds (occupied and unoccupied) to identify all outstanding maintenance needs including: drywall damage, plumbing fixtures (sinks, toilets, showers), flooring, furniture condition, paint, and safety hazards. A prioritized punch list was developed with target completion dates.
Linen: A facility-wide linen inventory and par-level assessment was completed. Each unit's supply was evaluated against census and ADL needs. Par levels were established at a minimum of 3x daily census requirements per unit.

3. Systemic Changes to Prevent Recurrence

Hot Water Temperature Management (effective immediately):

  • Thermostatic mixing valves installed on all hot water supply lines serving resident areas.
  • Daily water temperature monitoring logs implemented: Maintenance staff will test and record hot water temperatures at designated sentinel rooms (minimum 1 per unit) every morning. Target range: 100–110°F; maximum not to exceed 120°F.
  • Temperature log reviewed daily by Maintenance Director and weekly by the Administrator.
  • Any reading above 115°F triggers immediate investigation, adjustment, and notification to Administrator within 1 hour.

Environmental Maintenance Program (revised 3/10/2026):

  • Structured weekly environmental rounds conducted by the Maintenance Director with documentation on a standardized Environmental Rounds Checklist covering: walls/paint, plumbing fixtures, furniture, flooring, lighting, doors/thresholds, and safety hazards.
  • Monthly room-by-room inspections by Unit Managers in collaboration with Maintenance, with work orders generated for identified deficiencies.
  • A preventive maintenance schedule established for all plumbing fixtures, with sinks/toilets inspected quarterly and replaced per a rolling capital replacement plan.
  • Work order tracking system implemented with target completion timelines: safety hazards within 24 hours, comfort/cosmetic issues within 14 days.

Linen Management (revised 3/10/2026):

  • Par levels for linens, towels, washcloths, and personal care supplies established at 3x daily census per unit, posted in each linen storage area.
  • Weekly linen inventory conducted by Housekeeping Supervisor with reports to the Administrator.
  • Laundry service contract reviewed; turnaround time reduced from 48 hours to 24 hours with backup supplier identified.
  • Nursing staff educated to report linen shortages immediately to the charge nurse, who escalates to Housekeeping Supervisor same shift.

All Maintenance staff, Housekeeping staff, GNAs, licensed nurses, and department heads received in-service education by 3/15/2026 on environmental safety, reporting responsibilities, and the revised policies.

4. Monitoring

Hot Water:
Daily temperature monitoring logs reviewed by Maintenance Director (daily) and Administrator (weekly) for 12 weeks minimum.
Weekly random spot-check audits of water temperatures in 5 resident rooms per unit by the DON or designee for 12 weeks.
After 12 weeks of 100% compliance, spot-checks transition to monthly. Daily monitoring logs continue indefinitely.

Physical Environment:
The punch list will be reviewed weekly by the Administrator for progress. Target: all safety hazards resolved within 7 days; all cosmetic/comfort items within 60 days.
Weekly environmental rounds documentation reviewed by the Administrator for 12 weeks.
Monthly comprehensive room inspections for 6 months, then quarterly.

Linen:
Weekly linen inventory audits for 12 weeks, then monthly.
GNA and nurse satisfaction surveys regarding supply availability conducted monthly for 6 months.

All monitoring results reported to the QAPI Committee monthly. The SQC determination will be treated as a sentinel event with standing QAPI agenda item for 12 months. Non-compliance triggers immediate escalation to the Administrator and corporate leadership.
